Sci-Fi FPS Game

YEAR 2 SEM 1

Sci-Fi FPS Game

Overview

This project involved creating a game where the player, whose spaceship crashes on Mars, must retrieve a sacred crystal to save Earth. The player navigates the environment, collects items, battles enemies, and solves puzzles to complete the mission.

Role

Game Developer

C# Coding, Level Design

1 June 2023 - 2 July 2023



Background

The game's storyline follows a player's spaceship crash on an unknown planet, later identified as Mars. The mission is to retrieve a sacred gem or crystal, protected by aliens, to save Earth. The player must navigate obstacles, collect helpful items, defeat enemies, and return to the spaceship to win.

Tools Used

  • Unity
  • Visual Studio

Objective of the Game

  • The player is stranded on Mars and must complete a mission from headquarters to retrieve a sacred crystal.

  • Navigate obstacles and defeat enemies to collect the crystal.

  • Place the crystal in the access point to return to the spaceship and win the game.

Platforms & Hardware Desired Settings

  • Platforms: Windows and iOS
  • Required Memory Space: NIL

Game Implementation & Mechanics

The game allows players to collect items and weapons to defeat enemies. Players can jump to reach high items, and their health bar decreases when attacked. Upon collecting all necessary items and returning to the spaceship, a cutscene congratulates the player for winning.


To win, players must collect three items and a special crystal, navigate through a maze, and defeat enemies. After collecting all items, players place them in the access point to teleport back to the spaceship.

Player Controls

  • W:Move forward
  • S: Move backward
  • A: Move left
  • D: Move right
  • Spacebar: Jump
  • Mouse Movement: Camera control
  • K: Decrease health bar
  • Esc: Pause the game

Inspector Adjustments

I made several adjustments in the inspector, including changing enemy damage values, player health settings, enemy speed, sight range, detection range, and KO time.

Collectibles

  • Gun for defeating enemies
  • Grenade, chip, case, and the special crystal

Challenges and Solutions

The game faced some challenges, such as issues with item detection requiring multiple clicks and GitHub commit issues with large files. To address these, I increased detection sensitivity and managed file sizes by excluding larger files from commits.

Results & Takeaways

The game successfully integrates player controls, item collection, enemy combat, and puzzle-solving elements, creating an engaging and challenging experience. The use of Unity and Visual Studio Code ensured efficient development and smooth gameplay.

Some key takeaways I got from this project are:

This project enhanced my skills in using Unity and Visual Studio Code for game development. I gained experience in troubleshooting and optimizing game mechanics, improving my ability to create immersive and interactive environments. The process also highlighted the importance of thorough testing and iteration to achieve a polished final product.

3D Medieval Environment

Year 2 Sem 1

3D Medieval Environment

Overview

In this project, I created a detailed medieval environment from scratch using Maya. I designed both modular assets, like walls and roofs, which could be easily assembled, and hero assets, like a mystical crystal and a medieval table, which served as focal points. After building these assets, I imported them into Unity to check their look and functionality. I adjusted lighting, textures, and materials to create the desired atmosphere. The final result is a cohesive and immersive medieval environment that looks great in Unity.

Role

3D Modeler

Modelling, Texturing

17 April 2023 - 4 June 2023

Background

In this project, my goal was to create an immersive medieval environment from scratch using industry-standard tools and techniques. This involved designing and modeling various assets, including modular and hero assets, to be used in a real-time engine like Unity. The focus was on achieving a high level of detail and authenticity, with particular attention to key elements such as a mystical crystal.

Tools Used

  • Maya
  • Z-Brush
  • Adobe Substance Painter
  • Unity

Crystal Hero Asset

  • Low-Poly and High-Poly Versions: Both low-poly and high-poly versions of the crystal were created in Maya before moving on to texturing in Substance Painter. This step ensured that the crystal had the necessary detail for close-up views while maintaining performance efficiency.

  • Creation: The mystical crystal was crafted using Z-Brush, which allowed for detailed sculpting and shaping.

  • Texturing: After modeling, the crystal was textured using Adobe Substance Painter. This software enabled the application of realistic textures and materials to enhance the visual appeal.

  • Role:The crystal served as a hero asset, acting as a focal point in the environment and adding depth and interest to the scene.

Modelling Process In Maya

  • Initial Models:The initial models of the medieval buildings and structures were created in Maya before texturing. This stage involved basic shaping and structuring to define the overall look.

  • Whitebox:A whitebox version of the medieval building was created to visualize the layout and ensure proper proportions and placement of the assets within the environment.

Results & Takeaways

The final medieval environment, including the detailed modular and hero assets, was successfully integrated into Unity. The assets maintained their visual quality and performance, creating a cohesive and immersive scene. The use of realistic textures and materials, combined with appropriate lighting and atmospheric effects, resulted in a visually compelling environment.

Some key takeaways I got from this project are:

  • Tool Proficiency: Gained extensive experience with industry-standard tools such as Maya, Z-Brush, Adobe Substance Painter, and Unity.

  • Efficiency: Learned the importance of modular assets in streamlining the design process and facilitating rapid adjustments.

  • Detail & Realism: Developed skills in creating highly detailed and realistic textures and models.

  • Integration Understood the challenges and solutions involved in integrating assets into a real-time engine while maintaining visual quality and performance.

The Money Pit

YEAR 1 SEM 2

The Money Pit

Overview

In this project, I served as the Visual/Concept Designer tasked with designing the prototype for a new cardboard board game set to be launched in 2022. As part of a game companys creative team, my role was to craft a visually engaging and conceptually coherent design for the games assets, focusing on creating a prototype that captured the essence of the game without being a consumer-ready product.

Role

Visual/Concept Designer

Wireframing/Sketching, Designing

25 October 2022 - 30 November 2022

Background

The design concept for "The Money Pit" revolved around a casino theme. I aimed for a fun and minimalistic approach, drawing inspiration from classic casino aesthetics while infusing the design with a fresh, modern twist. The color scheme featured a vibrant mix of pastel tones with dominant reds and yellows, contrasted against black fonts and rich graphic elements to evoke the excitement of a casino environment.

Tools Used

  • Adobe Illustrator

Design Details

  • Visual Theme:I opted for a casino-inspired theme, which was reflected in the choice of colors, graphics, and overall design. The board game features a sleek, minimal design that balances vibrant casino colors with pastel backgrounds for a visually appealing look.
  • Color Scheme: The main colors used were red and yellow, chosen for their energetic and eye-catching qualities, with a background of pastel colors to soften the look. Black fonts and graphics provided clear readability and contrast.
  • Graphics:I created task cards and board spaces with dynamic, casino-themed graphics. The design included various graphic elements such as chips, dice, and playing cards, which were used to represent different game tasks and activities.
  • Poster Design: I also developed a promotional poster for "The Money Pit," which was designed to be visually cohesive with the board game. The poster maintained the same casino-inspired aesthetic, using similar colors and graphic elements to ensure brand consistency and attract potential players.
  • Software:The designs were crafted using Adobe Illustrator, allowing for precise vector illustrations and clean, scalable graphics.

Results&Takeaways

The prototype for "The Money Pit" effectively captured the excitement of a casino-themed board game through a vibrant, minimalistic design. The engaging aesthetic, characterized by a fun mix of red, yellow, and pastel colors with clear graphics, was well-received for its visual appeal and intuitive gameplay. The promotional poster also complemented the board games look, creating a cohesive brand identity that successfully attracted potential players. Overall, the project demonstrated my ability to balance aesthetic design with functionality and promotional effectiveness.

Some key takeaways I got from this project are:

  • Balance of Fun and Function:The project reinforced the importance of balancing aesthetic appeal with functional design. A visually engaging game can attract players, but clear and intuitive design is essential for a positive gameplay experience.
  • Color Theory: I gained practical experience in color theory, understanding how to use contrasting colors effectively to enhance visual impact and readability.
  • Graphic Design Skills:This project enhanced my skills in Adobe Illustrator, particularly in creating detailed vector graphics and integrating design elements into a cohesive visual theme.
  • Promotional Design: I learned valuable lessons in creating promotional materials that align with and amplify the games visual identity, ensuring consistency across different platforms and materials.

Overall, the project was a valuable learning experience that allowed me to apply my design skills in a practical context, resulting in a visually appealing and engaging board game prototype.
